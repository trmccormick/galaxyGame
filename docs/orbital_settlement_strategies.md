# Orbital Settlement Strategies

This document outlines the four primary orbital settlement protocols implemented by the AI Manager. Each protocol is designed for a specific planetary or system context, optimizing for resource availability, industrial capacity, and strategic expansion.

## 1. Mars-Style Protocol
- **Applicability:** Small moons, asteroids
- **Method:**
  - Initial capture and tug operations
  - Progressive hollowing for habitat and resource extraction
  - Modular expansion as population and industry grow
- **Key Features:**
  - Focus on scalable, low-gravity environments
  - Emphasis on resource mining and internal habitat construction

## 2. Earth/Luna-Style Protocol
- **Applicability:** Large moons (e.g., Luna), major planetary satellites
- **Method:**
  - Establish large-scale industry on the moon
  - Use lunar resources for orbital assembly and construction
  - Support for surface-to-orbit logistics and manufacturing
- **Key Features:**
  - Heavy industry and infrastructure development
  - Orbital shipyard and station assembly

## 3. No Moons Protocol
- **Applicability:** Planets without significant moons
- **Method:**
  - Capture and utilize asteroid belt resources
  - Construct orbital stations from imported or captured materials
  - Emphasis on distributed, modular station networks
- **Key Features:**
  - Reliance on asteroid mining and capture
  - Flexible station placement and expansion

## 4. Interplanetary Import Protocol
- **Applicability:** Systems with nearby resource-rich planets or belts
- **Method:**
  - Optimize supply chain from nearest system or planet
  - Distance-based logistics planning for resource import
  - Build orbital infrastructure using imported materials
- **Key Features:**
  - Advanced supply chain management
  - Dynamic resource routing and station construction

**Implementation Note:**
All protocols are managed by the AI Manager, which selects the optimal strategy based on system configuration, available resources, and long-term expansion goals. Protocol selection is locked at the architecture level and reflected in simulation logic.
