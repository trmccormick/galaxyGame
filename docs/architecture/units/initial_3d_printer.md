# Initial 3D Printer for Luna Settlement

## Unit Overview
The first 3D printer deployed for Luna ISRU is a rugged, modular additive manufacturing unit designed to process local regolith into structural components. It is optimized for day-one operations, requiring minimal imported feedstock and supporting rapid bootstrapping of surface infrastructure.

## Key Features
- **Feedstock:** Qualified lunar regolith (with additives as needed)
- **Products:** 3D-printed panels, I-beams, and basic structural elements
- **Operation:** Automated, with remote monitoring and basic self-cleaning
- **Power:** Solar or nuclear auxiliary (site-dependent)
- **Mobility:** Skid-mounted for repositioning at new dig sites

## Manufacturing Chain
1. **Input:** Harvested and qualified regolith (see ISRU Primer)
2. **Processing:** Regolith is sintered or fused layer-by-layer to form components
3. **Output:** Structural panels and I-beams for initial habitat and infrastructure assembly

## Operational Notes
- Regolith composition must be checked before printing; additives (e.g., binders, alloying agents) may be required for strength and printability
- Printer is designed for rapid deployment and field servicing
- All output is compatible with early Luna construction blueprints (see: 3d_printed_regolith_panel_mk1_bp, 3d_printed_ibeam_mk1_bp)
