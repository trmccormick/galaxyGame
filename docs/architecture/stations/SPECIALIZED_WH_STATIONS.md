# Specialized Wormhole Stations (WS & AWS)

## Overview
Specialized Wormhole Stations include both natural (WS) and artificial (AWS) facilities designed for wormhole stabilization, EM harvesting, and knowledge transfer. These stations are critical for network expansion and interstellar logistics.

---

## WS (Natural) vs. AWS (Artificial)
- **WS (Natural):** Deployed at natural wormhole exits, often as temporary EM/resource harvesting rigs. Not always eligible for permanent AWS upgrade (see wh-expansion.md for Scout/Harvest logic).
- **AWS (Artificial):** Permanent, high-mass stations built for long-term stabilization and network integration. Requires high System_ROI and resource yield.

---

## EM Harvesting Rigs & Knowledge Gates
- **EM Harvesting:** Both WS and AWS can be equipped for electromagnetic extraction, supporting energy and resource needs.
- **Knowledge Gates:** Pre-Snap stations serve as data/logistics hubs; Post-Snap, they become critical for reconnecting orphaned systems and transferring tech/experience.

---

## Snap Event & Stability
- **Sol-side L3 Stability:** Only possible with a major planetary counterbalance (e.g., Jupiter). Rock or synthetic stations at L3 can serve as temporary anchors but are not sufficient for long-term stability.
- **Exit-side Snapping:** Documented as a risk for stations lacking adequate counterbalance, leading to orphaned colonies and the need for AWS reconnection.
