# Synthetic Mega-Stations (Manufactured Facilities)

## Overview
Synthetic Mega-Stations are large-scale, manufactured structures built at key orbital locations (e.g., Earth-Moon L1, Mars L1) to serve as logistics, manufacturing, or research hubs. These stations are constructed from imported or ISRU materials and feature advanced automation and crew support.

---

## Earth-Moon L1 Staging Hub
- **Orbital Depot:** First-phase construction, focused on logistics, refueling, and resource processing. High automation, minimal crew.
- **Planetary Staging Hub:** Second-phase, heavy manufacturing and shipyard operations. High crew requirements, advanced manufacturing.
- **8.5M kg Builds:** Mega-stations can reach masses of 8.5 million kg, supporting hundreds of crew and large-scale operations.
- **Phase 1/2 Sequencing:** Construction begins with the depot, followed by the staging hub once logistics are established.

---

## Construction & Operations
- **Manufactured Components:** Built from lunar or imported materials, assembled in orbit.
- **Shipyard Operations:** Support for tug, cycler, and interplanetary ship construction.
- **Automation Levels:** Ranges from highly automated depots to human-rated manufacturing hubs.

---

## Integration with Industrial Chains
- **Mass Driver/Propellant Loops:** Integrated with lunar and planetary supply chains for efficient resource flow.
- **Lunar Space Elevator Dependencies:** Mega-stations serve as nodes for elevator construction and interplanetary foundry operations.
