# Converted Rock Stations (Asteroid/Moon Conversion)

## Overview
Converted Rock Stations are constructed by hollowing out asteroids or small moons (e.g., Phobos/Deimos) and reinforcing them for use as depots, habitats, or logistics hubs. This approach leverages local mass for radiation shielding and structural stability, minimizing the need for imported materials.

---

## Tug-Led Hollowing & Relocation
- **Bootstrap/Tug Logic:** Initial missions use tugs to relocate suitable asteroids or moons into stable orbits (Trojan, L3, or planetary orbit).
- **Hollowing-as-Propulsion:** The process of hollowing out the body produces slag, which is expelled as propellant to adjust the station's trajectory and final position.
- **Phobos/Deimos Patterns:** Standardized for Mars and Venus orbital infrastructure, using local moons or captured asteroids as the base.
- **Trojan-L3 Moves:** Stations can be positioned at Lagrange points for network integration or stability.

---

## Construction & Reinforcement
- **I-Beam Reinforcement:** Internal structures are printed or assembled using ISRU, providing support for habitats, tanks, and equipment.
- **Slag as Propellant:** Byproduct slag from hollowing is used for station maneuvering and orbit adjustments.
- **Radiation Shielding:** Natural mass provides superior protection compared to synthetic stations.

---

## Operational Patterns
- **Dumb Depots:** Minimal automation, used for storage and logistics with low maintenance.
- **EM Harvesting Rigs:** Upgraded rock stations equipped for electromagnetic resource extraction or as temporary wormhole anchors.

---

## Orphaned/Chat Logic Formalized
- **Hollowing-as-Propulsion:** Explicitly documented as a dual-purpose process for both station creation and maneuvering.
- **Sol-side L3 Stability:** Rock stations can be used as counterbalances at L3 points, but true stability requires a major planetary mass (see SPECIALIZED_WH_STATIONS.md).
