## Pre-Snap State

### The Sol-Eden Connection

The game begins after years of Sol-Eden colonization. Eden is not fully developed
but is an established settlement — populated, functional, economically connected
to Sol via the Natural Wormhole (NWH).

**Sol side**:
- NWH anchored by Jupiter — relatively stable, low EM output
- Limited EM research — not much instability to study
- AstroLift cyclers running Sol-Eden trade routes
- LDC managing colonial infrastructure

**Eden side**:
- NWH more active than Sol side — higher EM output
- Eden scientists are the leading EM researchers because of this
- More instability → more EM ejection → more data to study
- They are observing warning signs without fully understanding them
- Research data being shared with Sol regularly — but not yet interpreted

**The Irony**:
Eden's scientific advantage came FROM the instability that would destroy them.
They were studying the warning signs of their own isolation.
The answer to preventing the Snap was in their data.
Nobody recognized it in time.

## The Snap Event

The Eden NWH aperture destabilizes catastrophically.

**What happens**:
- Sol → Eden NWH connection severs completely
- Aperture shifts target — now connects Sol → System B (unknown, uncolonized)
- Eden is stranded with no connection to Sol
- Eden has whatever resources and people were on-planet at snap time
- AstroLift loses its Eden route overnight — economic shock
- LDC faces political crisis — a colony abandoned

**What Sol can immediately observe**:
- Looking through the shifted NWH into System B
- System B's NWH mouth is CALMER than Eden's was
- Low EM output, stable appearance
- The aperture snapped TO a more stable mass configuration
- This observation becomes the key to understanding everything

**What the AI Manager does**:
- Logs System B NWH stability metrics
- Notes Eden connection lost
- Recommends exploring System B as new opportunity
- Does NOT connect Eden's pre-Snap data to stabilization theory
- Continues optimizing logistics for the new situation
- Completely rational. Completely missing the point.

**The critical information gap**:
Eden's shared research data exists in Sol's systems.
The AI Manager has it, catalogued and filed.
It does not know this data is the key to reconnection.
It does not connect "Eden's EM readings" to "how to fix this."
A human player or researcher must make this connection.
This is the tutorial for EM research — not a prompt, a discovery.

## The Post-Snap Discovery

### Forensic Analysis

Sol scientists compare two datasets:
1. Eden's pre-Snap EM readings — accelerating instability, increasing output
2. System B NWH observations — calm, stable, low output

**The question**: Why is System B calm where Eden was not?

**The answer**: Mass distribution. System B's aperture mouth has better
natural mass anchoring than Eden's did. The NWH didn't snap randomly —
it sought gravitational equilibrium. It snapped TO stability.

**Secondary discovery**: Eden's increasing EM output WAS the warning.
Instability → more EM ejection → more energy loss → accelerating instability.
A feedback loop they had the data for but not the framework to interpret.

### The Counterbalance Rule — Discovered

```
Every NWH aperture requires sufficient mass anchoring on both sides.
Insufficient mass → aperture destabilizes → more EM ejected →
accelerating feedback → Snap.

Natural anchors (gas giants) are most efficient.
Harvested EM refocused at the aperture supplements insufficient mass.
Match the mass. Restore the equilibrium. Prevent the Snap.
```

Jupiter on Sol's side was always the stabilizing factor.
Eden's side lacked an equivalent mass anchor — chronic instability.
This is why Eden's NWH ejected more EM. This is why it Snapped.

### The Stabilization Technology — Too Late For Eden

Level 2 stabilization technology is now understood.
Cannot restore a wormhole that has already Snapped.
BUT: can be applied immediately to System B NWH to prevent another loss.
AND: provides the theoretical foundation for AWS construction.

## The LDC-AstroLift Collaboration

The Snap forces two rivals to work together.

**LDC** controls AWS technology development.
**AstroLift** controls the transport infrastructure and funding.
Neither can reconnect Eden alone.

The collaboration is uneasy. Both parties want credit, control, and
preferential terms after reconnection. Players operate in the middle
of this tension — can support either faction, play them against each other,
or build independent capability.

**The shared goal**: Reconnect Eden before the colony collapses.
**The hidden agenda**: Control the AWS network that results.

## Sol's AWS Build Sequence

### Phase 1 — Reconnect Eden

**AWS-Sol-1** constructed opposite Saturn.
Saturn's mass provides the counterbalance anchor.
Opens artificial connection → Eden coordinates (known, surveyed pre-Snap).

Stabilization satellites deployed at Eden aperture immediately.
Eden's NWH mouth is still unstable — sats buy time.
Eden must build a permanent anchor solution while sats hold.

**Eden's anchor options**:
- Survey Eden system for gas giant equivalent
  If found → position AWS to use it as natural anchor
- No suitable mass anchor found → Barbell Strategy
  Two AWS stations 180° apart in orbit
  Eden's star as central fulcrum
  More EM intensive — but Eden has the stockpile to fund it

**Eden's EM stockpile**:
During isolation Eden's unstable NWH ejected EM continuously.
Eden scientists harvested it the entire time they were stranded.
Eden has the largest EM stockpile in known space at reconnection.
What was a curse became an inheritance.
Eden does not need Sol's charity — Sol needs Eden's EM.

### Phase 2 — Open System B Artificially

System B is already being explored through the natural NWH.
But the natural NWH is unstable — could Snap again.
Need a stable artificial connection.

**AWS-Sol-2** or **AWS-Eden-2** opens artificial connection → System B.
Now two routes exist to System B:
- Route 1: Sol → System B via natural shifted NWH (free, unstable)
- Route 2: Sol → System B via AWS (paid, stable)

This tension is intentional. AstroLift wants the natural NWH to Snap —
it removes free competition to their AWS toll routes.
LDC wants it studied — best physics lab available.
Players who stabilize the natural NWH keep the free route open.
Players who let it Snap or trigger it deliberately open new connections.

### Phase 3 — Complete the Triangle

**AWS-Eden-3** completes the first network triangle:

```
        SOL
       /   \
 AWS-Sol-1  AWS-Sol-2
     /           \
  EDEN ─────── SYSTEM B
        AWS-Eden-3
         (+ natural NWH still open)
```

Three systems. Three artificial connections. One natural connection.
Eden is now the geographic center of known space.
Eden controls two of three inter-system AWS connections.
The power dynamic has completely reversed.

## The Controlled Snap Discovery

### The Experiment

With the triangle complete and all connections stable via AWS,
Sol scientists conduct a controlled experiment.

They remove the stabilization satellites from the System B NWH.
For the first time they observe an unstabilized aperture with
full data collection — unlike Eden which was unmonitored.

**What they discover**:
Without stabilization sats the NWH begins to destabilize predictably.
EM output increases — confirming the feedback loop model.
The aperture becomes mobile — seeking mass equilibrium elsewhere.

**The key finding**:
If sufficient mass is sent through the aperture at the right moment
in the decay cycle, the Snap can be triggered deliberately.
The mass accelerates instability and precipitates the shift.

**What this means**:
- WHEN it Snaps: controllable
- WHERE it Snaps to: NOT controllable
  Nature finds its own mass equilibrium
  Destination determined by regional mass distribution
  Cannot be steered — only triggered

### System C — An Educated Gamble

Long-range surveys had already identified System C as a likely
mass concentration in the region beyond System B.

Sol scientists calculate the probability that a triggered Snap
from System B would shift toward System C.
They decide the probability is acceptable.
They send mass through — asteroid relocation tugs deliver payload.
The Snap triggers.
System C connection opens.

It was not precision. It was the best available bet.
The skill of reading mass distribution data to predict Snap destinations
becomes a valuable and rare expertise.

### Political Consequences

The controlled Snap discovery changes everything.

**Anyone with asteroid relocation tugs and sufficient mass
can trigger a controlled Snap of any natural wormhole.**

This means:
- Natural wormholes are now strategic vulnerabilities
- A rogue actor could redirect Sol's NWH away from System B
- Eden could redirect their remaining unstable NWH deliberately
- Asteroid relocation tugs are reclassified as strategic military assets
- LDC pushes for tug licensing and regulation
- AstroLift wants tugs controlled — protects their AWS investment
- Eden uses tug capability as political leverage
- Players with tugs have significant power

## Faction Dynamics Post-Reconnection

### Sol
Controls Jupiter-anchored NWH (now pointing at System B).
Built the first AWS — expects credit and preferred access.
Wants Eden gratitude. Won't get it.
Home of LDC — pushing for AWS technology licensing control.

### Eden
EM rich — largest stockpile in known space.
Years of isolated research — may have advanced beyond Sol in some areas.
Geographically central — controls two of three triangle connections.
Wants autonomy. Was abandoned. Will not forget.
Has tug capability and EM leverage.

### System B
New frontier. Explored through Sol's shifted NWH.
First settlers arrived before Eden was even reconnected.
No established faction loyalty — wild card.
Whoever controls System B's AWS controls the next expansion phase.

### AstroLift
Cycler network disrupted by Snap — lost Eden routes overnight.
Now rebuilding with AWS network as backbone.
Wants to monetize all transit — AWS tolls, portal fees.
Threatened by natural NWH free routes AND future warp drive.
Adapts by moving into premium express services as tech advances.

### LDC
Controls AWS technology development — wants licensing fees.
Wants to prevent unauthorized tug-triggered Snaps.
In uneasy collaboration with AstroLift.
Losing control as Eden and System B develop independent capability.

### Players
Operate in the middle of all faction tensions.
Can align with any faction, play them against each other,
or build independent capability outside faction control.
Tug ownership, EM stockpiles, portal gate placement,
and AWS construction access are the primary power levers.
