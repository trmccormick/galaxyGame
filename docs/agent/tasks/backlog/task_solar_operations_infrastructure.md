# Solar System Infrastructure - Solar Monitoring & Advanced Operations

## Overview
Establish solar monitoring, coronal mass ejection (CME) early warning systems, and advanced solar technologies around the Sun, complementing Venus's solar power generation with safety and propulsion systems.

## Scientific Basis
- **Coronal Mass Ejections**: Solar activity requiring monitoring/protection
- **Solar Wind**: Constant particle stream affecting operations
- **Solar Sails**: Propulsion technology testing ground
- **Solar Physics**: Fundamental research opportunities

## Game Integration
**Role**: Solar System safety and advanced technology infrastructure
- **Early Warning**: CME monitoring and prediction systems
- **Research**: Solar physics and space weather studies
- **Propulsion**: Solar sail testing and development (Venus handles power generation)
- **Safety Systems**: Protection from solar activity disruptions

## Mission Structure
1. **CME Monitoring Network** - Deploy detection and warning systems
2. **Solar Sail Testing** - Develop and test solar propulsion technologies
3. **Solar Physics Research** - Establish research facilities for solar studies
4. **System Integration** - Connect with Venus power infrastructure

## Strategic Value
- **Safety Systems**: Protection from solar activity disruptions
- **Technological Advancement**: Solar sail technology for efficient travel
- **Research Hub**: Solar physics research and space weather studies
- **System Protection**: Early warning systems for all space infrastructure

- **Development Timeline**: Phase 4-8 implementation (infrastructure foundation)
